# Product Vision

Status: Draft  
Last updated: 2026-06-26

## Vision

Signal helps a senior engineer who is not actively job hunting discover exceptional career opportunities without spending hours searching, filtering, and researching.

The system continuously monitors the job market, builds knowledge about companies, evaluates opportunities against the candidate's preferences, and prepares clear briefings when something deserves attention.

## The user problem

The user is comfortable in their current job.

They are not unemployed.
They are not desperate.
They are not willing to apply everywhere.

However, they do not want to miss an exceptional opportunity.

The problem is that finding exceptional opportunities requires time, context, and energy:

- identifying high-quality companies;
- understanding culture and benefits;
- filtering out poor-fit roles;
- adapting resumes and messages;
- deciding the best way to enter a company;
- tracking follow-ups.

This work is valuable, but it competes with the user's current job and life.

## Product thesis

A career intelligence system can do the heavy work continuously and selectively surface only the opportunities worth human attention.

The system should make the user feel:

> "I am not actively job hunting, but I am not missing the right opportunity."

## Desired outcome

Within six months, the user should be able to say:

> "I got an incredible job because of the tool I built."

Success means helping the user find a company with:

- excellent culture;
- strong compensation;
- meaningful benefits;
- an interesting product;
- strong technical standards;
- a role aligned with their frontend and AI interests;
- a work environment they would genuinely enjoy.

## What makes Signal different

Signal is not optimized for application volume.

It is optimized for judgment.

The differentiator is depth before interruption:

1. discover;
2. research;
3. understand;
4. evaluate;
5. strategize;
6. prepare;
7. only then interrupt.

The system should not say "look at this job" too early.

It should say:

> "I investigated this company, compared it against your preferences, found a viable strategy, and I believe this deserves your attention."

## Product posture

Signal should feel like a thoughtful career analyst, not a noisy assistant.

It should be:

- selective;
- calm;
- explainable;
- honest;
- evidence-based;
- respectful of attention;
- useful even when it recommends doing nothing.
